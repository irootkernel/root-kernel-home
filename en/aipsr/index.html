Speed & Cost

A faster, lower-cost research flow than conventional surveys

Traditional surveys and polling require recruiting real respondents, collecting responses, and analyzing raw data, which can mean at least several days of lead time plus labor, panel, and incentive costs. AIPSR is designed so teams can ask questions conversationally with a Synthetic Audience, branch into follow-up questions based on responses, and extend hard-to-contact segments at lower unit cost.

ComparisonConventional surveys and pollingAIPSR research flow
SpeedRecruiting respondents, offline contact, online panel operation, and raw-data cleanup can take two to three days or more.A configured Synthetic Audience can split preference and rejection segments by response, continue with follow-up questions, and compare survey drafts, messages, and design options repeatedly.
CostLabor, panel recruitment, respondent incentives, and analysis time accumulate with each study.A reusable persona pool lowers the unit cost of additional survey runs and segment expansion.
ReachSegments with narrow conditions or hard-to-contact people can be slow and expensive to assemble.Reachable human responses can stay in the study, while underrepresented or hard-to-contact segments are extended or partially replaced with AI Persona responses.
IterationChanging questions or design options often means restarting recruitment, data collection, and analysis.Teams can ask new questions to the Synthetic Audience and compare preference, rejection reasons, and improvement directions across changed messages and design variants.

Difference

How Root Kernel differs from other AI Persona Synthesis Research

Root Kernel does not rely only on public statistics or a small set of expensive panel interviews. It aims to build AI Personas from preference data collected through Sudal's image-based balance games and repeated A/B choices, including visual taste signals.

ApproachLimitRoot Kernel's view
Public-statistics personasThey can describe group averages such as age, gender, and region, but they are weak at real taste and design preference.Public statistics are used for group baselines and weighting, while preference judgment is grounded in actual choice data.
Panel-interview personasThey can be deep, but they are expensive, hard to repeat, and can depend too heavily on a small number of respondents.Sudal is designed to accumulate repeated choice data more naturally through balance games and A/B choices.
Root Kernel AI PersonasA demographic profile alone is not enough. Real choice patterns and visual taste should be connected to the persona.Personas can be connected not only to text preferences, but also to visual preference signals for color, mood, design, UI, and package images.

Behavior Simulation

Grounded Agent Simulation

Human Behavior Simulation

Stanford HAI-related research shows that AI agents grounded in interviews and self-reports from 1,052 real people can show response patterns similar to the original participants on some survey tasks. The important point is simple: AI personas become more useful when they are grounded in rich human data, not when they are invented from scratch.

Limits & Guardrails

Cohort Priors & Safe Positioning

Public Statistics & Guidelines

Root Kernel does not claim that every human response should be replaced by AI. Public statistics are used only for group-level baselines and weighting, not to label individuals. AIPSR is designed as research infrastructure that can combine reachable human responses with synthetic responses from AI Personas for segments that are hard to contact or underrepresented.
